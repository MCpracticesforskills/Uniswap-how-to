The Basics of Uniswap, Explained

Yes, I realize decentralized finance is scary at first, but once you understand it you will love it. I promise.

Okay, so Uniswap is an automated, decentralized liquidity pool. What does that mean?
Automated means that transactions occur online. Decentralized means the liquidity pool
is not subject to a central authority. There are no listing fees or listing proccesses.
Traders exchange tokens on a protocol that runs on the Etherium network. The beauty of a
liquidity pool is anyone can lend their cryptocurrency regardless of credit score,
financial assets, or other bank-arbitrated requirements. In exchange for providing money
to a pool, or multiple pools, traders earn fees.

How Does Uniswap Work?üß†

First of all, it is important to understand that there are no order books. Liquidity pools use
smartcontracts called Constant Product Market Maker. (You don't have to know the name of the
Smart Contract, but it sounds fancy if you want to impress your friends with your decentralized
finance knowledge). Smartcontracts hold liquidity pools that people trade against. Here are the rules:

  • liquidity pools are funded by liquidity providers who earn currency from fees
  • ANYONE can be a liquidity provider IF they can deposit two tokens into the pool (either 2 ERC-20 tokens OR 1ETC token+1ERC-20 token)
  • traders pay a fee to the pool üíµ
  • fees are then distributed to liquidity providers according to their share of the pool (the more somone provides, the higher percentage he/she earns
  • liquidity pools are usually made up of stablecoins üí≤
